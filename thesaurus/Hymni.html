Hymns for various feasts and seasons.
  • Tempus per Annum (Ordinary Time)
    • Ad Primam (Prime, first hour of the day, sunrise)
      • Iam Lucis Orto Sidere (Star of Light Now Having Risen) (Breviarium Romanum)
    • Ad Tertiam, Sextam, et Nonam (Daytime hours, terce, sext and nones)
      • Nunc, Sancte nobis, Spiritus (Ad Tertiam, ~9:00am)
      • Rector Potens, verax Deus (Ad Sextam, ~noon)
      • Rerum, Deus, tenax vigor (Ad Nonam~3:00pm)
    • Ad Completorium (Compline or Nighttime Prayer)
      • Te lucis ante terminum (Before the End of Light)
      • Christe, qui, splendor et dies (Liturgia Horarum)
    • Die Dominica (Sunday)
      • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
        • Nocte surgentes (Now From the Slumbers of the Night) (Breviarium Romanum)
        • Primo dierum omnium (First of All Days)
        • Primo die, quo Trinitas
      • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
        • Aeterne rerum conditor (Maker of all, eternal King)
        • Ecce iam noctis (Lo! The Dim Shadows of the Night)
      • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
        • Lucis Creator optime ( O blest Creator of the light)
        • O lux beata Trinitas (O Trinity of Blessed Light)
    • Feria Secunda (Monday)
      • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
        • Somno refectis artubus ( Our Limbs Refreshed with Slumber Now)
        • Aeterna lux, divinitas (Eternal Light, Divinity) (Liturgia Horarum)
      • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
        • Splendor Paternae gloriae (O Splendor of God's Glory)
      • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
        • Immense caeli Conditor (O great Creator of the Sky)
    • Feria Tertia (Tuesday)
      • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
        • Consors Paterni luminis (O Light of Light)
        • Nocte surgentes (Now From the Slumbers of the Night) (Liturgia Horarum)
      • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
        • Ales diei nuntius (The Winged Herald of the Day) (Breviarium Romanum)
      • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
        • Telluris ingens Conditor (Earth's Mighty Maker)
        • Telluris alme Conditor
    • Feria Quarta (Wednesday)
      • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
        • Rerum Creator optime (Who madest all)
      • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
        • Nox, et tenebrae, et nubila (Day is breaking, dawn is bright)
      • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
        • Caeli Deus sanctissime (O God, Whose Hand Hath Spread the Sky)
    • Feria Quinta (Thursday)
      • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
        • Nox atra rerum contegit (The Dusky Veil of Night)
        • Ales diei nuntius (The Winged Herald of the Day) (Liturgia Horarum)
      • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
        • Lux ecce surgit aurea (See the Golden Sun Arise) (Breviarium Romanum)
        • Sol ecce surgit igneus (Liturgia Horarum)
        • Iam Lucis Orto Sidere (Star of Light Now Having Risen) (Liturgia Horarum)
      • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
        • Magnae Deus potentiae (O Sovereign Lord of Nature's Might)
    • Feria Sexta (Friday)
      • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
        • Tu, Trinitas Unitas (O Three in One, and One in Three)
      • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
        • Aeterna caeli gloria ( O Christ, Whose Glory Fills the Heaven)
      • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
        • Plasmator hominis, Deus (Maker of Man)
        • Hominis superne Conditor
    • Sabbato (Saturday)
      • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
        • Summae Deus clementiae (Great God of Boundless Mercy) (Liturgia Horarum)
        • Summae Parens clementiae (Breviarium Romanum)
      • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
        • Aurora iam spargit polum (The Dawn is Sprinkling in the East)
      • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
        • Iam sol recedit igneus (As Fades the Glowing Orb of Day)
        • Deus creator omnium (God That All Things Didst Create) (Liturgia Horarum)
      • Tempus Adventus (Advent)
        • Ante Diem 16 Decembris
          • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
            • Conditor Alme Siderum (Creator of the Stars of Night)
            • Creator alme siderum (Bright Builder of the Heavenly Poles)
          • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
            • Verbum supernum prodiens (Celestial Word, to This Our Earth)
          • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
            • Vox clara ecce intonat (A Thrilling Voice by Jordan Rings)

        • Post Diem 16 Decembris

          • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
            • Verbum salutis omnium
          • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)

          • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)

    • Sacruum Triduum Paschale Passionis et Resurrectionis Domini (The Triduum)
      • Note: In the 1962 Roman Breviary, no hymns were sung for the various hours during the Triduum, nor even during Easter Week. The hymns for the hours below are part of the post-SVC Liturgy of the Hours and are, in fact, quite ancient. Unfortunately I do not have translations for many of them, so I have omitted all but the ones I do have translations for. Perhaps someday I will put the Latin on the web so at least it will be available.
      • Feria Quinta in Cena Domini (Holy Thursday/ Last Supper)
        • Pange Lingua (Sing, My Tongue), Aquinas
        • Ubi Caritas
        • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
          • O memoriale mortis Domini (O Memorial of my Savior Dying)

      • Feria Sexta in Passione Domini (Good Friday)
        • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
          • Pange, lingua, gloriosi (Sing, my Tongue)
        • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
          • En acetum, fel, arundo (Lo, with Gall His thirst He Quenches!)
        • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
          • Vexilla regis prodeunt (Royal Banners)

      • Sabbato Sancto (Holy Saturday)
        • Ad Officium lectionis (Readings / Matins)
          • Christe, caelorum Domine
        • Ad Laudes matutinas (Lauds)
          • Tibi, Redemptor omnium
        • Ad Vesperas (Vespers)
          • Auctor salutis unice
        • Exsultet
