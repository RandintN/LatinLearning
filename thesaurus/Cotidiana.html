Dedication of the Months of the Year

It has been a long standing practice to focus on a particular feast or devotion during each month of the year as an aid to meditation on its subject and to cultivate sanctity. Since many months contain several important feasts, there is more than one possibility for them. The list given below are the most common monthly dedications.

  • Ianuarius (January)
    • Sanctissimum Nomen Iesu (Holy Name)
      (By virtue of the feast of the Circumcision on January 1)
    • Iesu Infans (Infant Jesus)
      (By virtue of the Christmas Season and the Circumcision on January 1 )
  • Februarius (February)
    • Sancta Trinitas (Holy Trinity)
    • Sancta Familia (Holy Family)
      (By virture of the Feast of the Presentation and Purification on February 2)
  • Martius (March)
    • Sanctus Ioseph (St. Joseph)
      (By virtue of the Feast of St. Joseph on March 19)
  • Aprilis (April)
    • Sanctissimum Sacramentum (Blessed Sacrament)
      (By virtue of Holy Thursday usually falling in April)
    • Spiritus Sanctus (Holy Spirit)
      (By virtue of the upcoming feast of Pentecost)
    • Passio Christi (Passion of Christ)
      (By virtue of Holy Week usually falling in April)
  • Maius (May)
    • Beata Virgo Maria (Blessed Virgin Mary)
      (By long standing tradition)
  • Iunius (June)
    • Sacratissimum Cor Iesu (Most Sacred Heart of Jesus)
      (By virtue of the Feast of the Sacred Heart, which often falls in June)
    • Sanctissimum Sacramentum (Blessed Sacrament)
      (By virtue of the Feast of Corpus Christi usually falling in June)
  • Iulius (July)
    • Pretiosissimus Sanguis (Precious Blood)
      (By virtue of the Feast of the Precious Blood, formerly on July 1)
  • Augustus (August)
    • Immaculatum Cor Mariae (Immaculate Heart of Mary)
      (By virtue of the Feasts of the Assumption and Queenship)
  • September
    • Sancta Crux (Holy Cross)
      (By virtue of Feast of the Holy Cross on September 14)
    • Beata Maria Virgo Perdolens (Sorrowful Mother)
      (By virture of the Feast of the Sorrowful Mother on September 15)
  • October
    • Beata Maria Virgo a Rosario (Our Lady of the Rosary)
      (By virtue of the feast of Our Lady of the Rosary on October 7)
    • Sancti Angeli (Holy Angels)
      (By virtue of the Feast of the Guardian Angels on October 2)
  • November
    • Omnium Fidelium Defunctorum (Holy Souls in Purgatory)
      (By virtue of the Feast of All Souls on November 2)
  • December
    • Immaculata Conceptio Beatae Mariae Virginis (Immaculate Conception)
      (By virtue of the Feast of the Immaculate Conception on December 8)
