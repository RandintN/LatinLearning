Seven Litanies to St. Benedict (Haeften, 1644)		 SBenedictusAb


Benedict van Haeften (Haeftenus) was a Benedictine writer and provost of the Monastery of Afflighem in Belgium. He was born at Utrecht in 1588 and died on 31 July 1648, at Spa, Belgium. He assembled the Litany below from titles given to St Benedict by various authors down through the centuries and thus is an important reference to the origins of many of the titles that appear in Litanies to St Benedict. Haeften's Litany is divided into seven parts, one part for each day of the week. The text below is taken from Haeften's Disquisitionum Monasticarum, Libri XII, Antverpiae apud Petrum Bellerum, 1644.

Kyrie Eleison
R. Kyrie Eleison
 
Christe Eleison
R. Christe Eleison
 
Kyrie Eleison
R. Kyrie Eleison
 
CHRISTE tuas, bone Pastor, oves NOS, quaesumus AUDI,
EXAUDI nostras CHRISTE benigne preces.
CAELESTIS MISERERE PATER, misererier usque
Cui proprium, et miseris nolle negare Patrem.
SALVATOR MUNDI, MISERERE, ter optime, FILI
De Genitrice hominis, de Genitore Dei
Patris amor, natique Patris, sed utrique coaeve,
SPIRITUS ALME DEUS, fons, MISERERE, boni.
SANCTA, hominum MISERERE, TRIAS, DEUS in tribus UNUS.
O Pater ! o Fili! o Spiritus adfer opem!
 
SANCTISSIMA VIRGO MARIA,
SANCTA DEI GENETRIX.
R. Ora pro nobis.
 
CHRISTI purissima MATER,
VIRGINITATIS APEX.
R. Ora pro nobis.
